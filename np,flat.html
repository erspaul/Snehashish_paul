Exploring the Flat Areas of Nepal

Nepal's flat areas, located in the southern Terai region, offer a contrasting landscape to the mountainous and hilly terrains. The Terai is a vast, fertile plain extending from the foothills of the Himalayas to the border with India, known for its lush greenery and abundant agricultural fields.

The Terai is characterized by dense jungles, wildlife sanctuaries, and meandering rivers. This region is a habitat for diverse flora and fauna, including endangered species like the one-horned rhinoceros and Bengal tiger. Agriculture plays a major role, with extensive rice paddies and wheat fields contributing to the country's food production.

Explore national parks, enjoy wildlife safaris, and experience the vibrant festivals celebrated by the local Tharu communities. The flat areas of Nepal provide a different perspective, showcasing the country's biodiversity and the harmonious coexistence of nature and human life.
