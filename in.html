Explore Incredible India

Taj Mahal, Agra

The iconic Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is a symbol of eternal love. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, the white marble mausoleum is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City. Explore the historical City Palace, marvel at the architecture of Hawa Mahal, and visit the impressive Amer Fort overlooking the city.

Kerala Backwaters

Experience the tranquility of the Kerala Backwaters, a network of lakes, rivers, and canals. Enjoy a houseboat cruise through the scenic landscapes surrounded by coconut trees and traditional villages.

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, is a spiritual hub on the banks of the Ganges River. Witness the Ganga Aarti, explore ancient temples, and absorb the spiritual atmosphere.

Goa

Goa, known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife, is a popular tourist destination. Relax on the sandy shores, explore colonial architecture, and enjoy the unique blend of Indian and Portuguese culture.

Mysuru, Karnataka

Visit Mysuru to explore the majestic Mysore Palace, a royal residence with exquisite architecture. Experience the vibrant culture during the Dasara festival and explore the lush gardens and historic sites.

Rishikesh and Haridwar (Uttarakhand)

These twin cities along the Ganges River are known for their spiritual significance, yoga retreats, and adventure activities like white-water rafting.

Mumbai (Maharashtra)

India's financial capital, Mumbai, offers a mix of historical landmarks, bustling markets, and a vibrant film industry. Don't miss the Gateway of India and Marine Drive.

Delhi

The capital city of India, Delhi is a blend of historical monuments, bustling markets, and modern infrastructure. Key attractions include the Red Fort, India Gate, and Qutub Minar.

Khajuraho (Madhya Pradesh)

Known for its intricately carved temples depicting various forms of art and love, Khajuraho is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Amritsar (Punjab)

Home to the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), Amritsar is a spiritual and cultural center for the Sikh community.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves (Maharashtra)

These ancient rock-cut caves showcase exquisite sculptures and paintings, depicting Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain art.

Hampi (Karnataka)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi is a unique destination with ancient ruins, boulders, and a fascinating history.
